The conversation centers on developing, validating, and refining a manual, free-to-execute intraday relative-value trading strategy across precious metals (XAUUSD, XAGUSD, XPTUSD), with the core objective of exploiting short-horizon mean reversion during periods of volatility expansion combined with correlation stress, not trend-following or breakout trading. The strategy is explicitly hierarchical: correlation acts as the gatekeeper, volatility expansion as the trigger, and relative dislocation (Z-score spread) as the direction selector. Trades are only considered when average rolling correlation across the three metal pairs drops below ~0.65 (ideal 0.35–0.55, no trades above ~0.7), confirming structural decoupling. Volatility must expand in at least two of the three metals, proxied by ATR(14) exceeding its recent baseline (roughly ≥1.2×), ensuring overreaction rather than noise. Direction is determined by ranking Z-scores computed as (price – 20-period SMA) / 20-period standard deviation; the highest Z is sold and the lowest Z is bought, with a minimum Z-score spread of ~1.5 (ideal ≥2.0) to confirm meaningful relative mispricing. Platinum’s role is clarified as a valid laggard or leader only when its volatility is active; buying a metal with collapsing ATR is filtered out. Execution is strictly basket-based, simultaneous long/short entries within ~60 seconds, during specific liquidity windows (primarily London open and, secondarily, Asian volatility hours), avoiding major macro news and unified macro-driven regimes where correlation remains high. Risk management is fixed and simple: basket risk capped at ~1% of account, profit target ~1.0–1.2%, with edge coming from win rate and diversification rather than large R:R; individual legs share identical stops and targets conceptually, but exits are governed by total basket P&L. A hard time stop (~4 hours maximum) is critical, as empirical review showed convergence usually occurs quickly and overstaying trades degrades expectancy. Additional exits include partial convergence (Z-spread compressing below ~0.6–0.8) or correlation snapping back upward. Trade logs and reconstructed examples confirmed that valid signals occurred when ATR expansion and Z-score divergence aligned, but losses clustered where correlation never truly broke, volatility was one-sided, Z-spreads were marginal, or trades were held too long. Normalizing P&L by ATR highlighted the necessity of volatility-adjusted sizing and clean exit rules. Due to TradingView free-plan constraints (only two indicators per chart and Pine Script limitations), the workflow emphasizes free tools: MT4/MT5 for execution and indicator display, spreadsheets (Excel or Google Sheets) for calculating rolling correlations, ATR ratios, and Z-scores using imported price data (via broker exports, DDE, CSV, or manual logging), and minimal on-chart indicators (ATR plus either StdDev or Bollinger Bands with SMA) to approximate Z-score visually. Automation was intentionally deprioritized; EA logic was reviewed and deemed inferior to disciplined manual execution, as the true edge is contextual judgment around correlation regimes, volatility balance, and time decay rather than mechanical signal generation. The final consensus is that the strategy is fundamentally sound as a manual, rule-driven relative-value system, with success dependent on strict adherence to the hierarchy, disciplined risk and time exits, avoidance of macro-unified regimes, and continuous post-trade review rather than additional indicators or automation.
