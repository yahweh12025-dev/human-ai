Technical Critique:
- The circuit breaker uses fixed thresholds (5 consecutive losses, 10% daily loss, 15% drawdown) without dynamic adjustment, leading to premature halts or insufficient protection during high‑volatility periods.
- Position sizing in `_apply_market_intelligence_sizing` only multiplies quantity by a sentiment factor, ignoring volatility and equity drawdown, which can cause over‑leveraged trades.
- Peak equity for drawdown calculation is never reset, so a sustained equity rise can mask later drawdowns, delaying the 15% limit trigger.
- Daily loss limit applies to cumulative `daily_pnl` across all symbols; a large winning trade can offset losses, making the limit ineffective at preventing equity erosion.
- The volatility spike check is incomplete (commented out), leaving the circuit breaker blind to rapid volatility changes.

Proposed Mathematical Improvement:
Modify position sizing to include a volatility‑adjusted multiplier that scales size inversely with market volatility:

```python
# Desired risk per trade as a fraction of current equity (e.g., 1%)
risk_per_trade = 0.01 * self.circuit_breaker_state['current_equity']

# Stop‑loss distance in price units
stop_loss_distance = abs(entry_price - stop_loss_price)

# Base quantity based on risk budget
base_quantity = risk_per_trade / stop_loss_distance

# Volatility scaling: reduce size when volatility is high
volatility_factor = 1.0 + 0.5 * (self.circuit_breaker_state['volatility_measure'] / self._average_volatility)
adjusted_quantity = base_quantity * self._sentiment_multiplier / volatility_factor
```

This keeps the actual dollar risk (risk_per_trade) constant while dynamically shrinking position size as volatility increases, aligning risk with market conditions.
