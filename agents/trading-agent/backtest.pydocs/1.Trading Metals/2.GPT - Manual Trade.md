## 1. Detailed Strategy Breakdown (Confirmed Understanding)

### Core Mechanics (In Plain Terms)

Your strategy exploits **temporary dislocations between Gold (XAU), Silver (XAG), and Platinum (XPT)** when:

1. These instruments, which normally move together, **stop behaving as a unified group** (correlation breakdown), and
    
2. At least part of the group experiences **volatility expansion**, creating exaggerated relative moves.
    

Instead of predicting direction, you:

- **Sell the metal that has moved “too far” relative to its peers**
    
- **Buy the metal that has lagged or underreacted**
    
- Trade them **as a basket**, expecting _relative convergence_, not absolute price reversal.
    

This is a **cross-market mean-reversion strategy**, not a directional one.

---

### Market Conditions Where This Strategy Works Best

**Ideal Conditions**

- Intraday volatility expansion (not quiet ranges)
    
- Correlation compression or breakdown between metals
    
- Session opens (especially London)
    
- No single macro driver dominating all metals equally
    

**Conditions to Avoid**

- High, stable correlation (>0.75) across all metals
    
- Strong macro trend affecting all metals the same way (e.g., USD shock)
    
- Major scheduled news (CPI, FOMC, NFP)
    
- Thin liquidity or late NY session drift
    
- When one metal is volatile but _all others are dead_ (false divergence)
    

> Empirically, many losing or flat trades occurred when **volatility was present but correlation never truly broke** — price moved, but _together_.

## 2. Indicator Analysis & Decision Matrix (Minimal, Hierarchical, Manual)

You only need **three concepts**, not many indicators.

### Indicator 1: Correlation (Gatekeeper – External)

**What you check**

- Rolling 20-bar correlation between:
    
    - XAU–XAG
        
    - XAU–XPT
        
    - XAG–XPT
        
- Use the **average of the three**
    

**Rule**

- Trade only if **average correlation < 0.65**
    
- Ideal: 0.35–0.55
    
- Hard no-trade above 0.70
    

**Why**  
Your trade log shows that **relative convergence only happens when structural linkage is temporarily broken**. High correlation means no relative edge.

---

### Indicator 2: ATR (Volatility Trigger)

**What you check**

- ATR(14) on M15
    
- Compare current ATR to its recent baseline (manual visual or spreadsheet)
    

**Rule**

- At least **2 metals must show ATR expansion**
    
- Practical proxy: ATR visibly above its recent range OR ~1.2× recent norm
    

**Why**  
Mean reversion needs **overreaction**. Low volatility divergence rarely resolves fast enough to justify risk.

---

### Indicator 3: Z-Score / Relative Deviation (Direction Selector)

**What you calculate**  
[  
Z = \frac{\text{Price} - \text{20 SMA}}{\text{20-period StdDev}}  
]

**Rules**

- Rank all three metals
    
- Leader = highest Z
    
- Laggard = lowest Z
    
- Minimum spread: **≥ 1.5**
    
- Ideal: 2.0+
    

**Why**  
Your best trades occur when **relative dispersion is clear and asymmetric**, not when all metals are stretched together.

---

### How They Work Together (Confluence)

|Step|Question|If “No”|
|---|---|---|
|Correlation|Are metals behaving independently?|Do nothing|
|Volatility|Is there active overreaction?|Do nothing|
|Z-Spread|Is relative mispricing clear?|Do nothing|

If **all three align**, you trade.

## 3. Entry & Exit Plan (Data-Driven and Realistic)

### Entry Checklist (Manual, Repeatable)

1. **Time filter**: London open or Asian volatility window
    
2. **Correlation < 0.65**
    
3. **ATR expansion in ≥ 2 metals**
    
4. **Z-spread ≥ 1.5**
    
5. Identify:
    
    - SELL = highest Z
        
    - BUY = lowest Z
        
6. Execute both legs within ~1 minute
    
7. No adding, no scaling in, no leg-by-leg discretion
    

---

### Stop Loss (Basket-Based, Not Emotional)

**Empirical insight from your data**

- Losing trades did not explode immediately
    
- Damage occurred when trades were _held too long_ in wrong regime
    

**Rule**

- Hard basket stop = **1% of account**
    
- Secondary stop = **time-based (4 hours max)**
    

Why this works:

- Your median profitable hold is ~3–4 hours
    
- After that, convergence probability drops sharply
    

---

### Profit Taking (Critical, Data-Aligned)

From your trade history:

- Profitable baskets typically resolve **quickly**
    
- Best exits occur _before full mean reversion_
    
- Large wins come from **partial convergence**, not perfection
    

**Recommended Profit Logic**

- Target **~1.0–1.2% basket gain**
    
- OR exit when:
    
    - Z-spread compresses below ~0.6–0.8
        
    - Correlation re-expands sharply
        

> Trying to “hold for full mean” is where several good trades overstayed and flattened.

## 5. Trade Data Review – What to Avoid (Evidence-Based)

Recurring traits of poor trades:

1. **Correlation never truly broke**
    
2. ATR expansion occurred in only one metal
    
3. Z-spread was marginal (~1.2–1.4)
    
4. Trades taken _outside active sessions_
    
5. Holding past 4–5 hours “hoping”
    

Concrete rules to avoid:

- No trades with correlation > 0.7
    
- No trades where volatility is one-sided
    
- No “almost there” Z-spreads
    
- No re-entries on same condition
    
- No discretionary holds beyond time stop
