Our entire conversation has focused on reverse-engineering and systematizing a precious metals basket trading strategy from provided trade history, aiming to create a manual trading framework and eventual Expert Advisor (EA) for a $5,000 account. The core objective was to decode a relative value mean-reversion strategy that exploits temporary correlation breakdowns during volatility spikes across XAUUSD, XAGUSD, and XPTUSD, not directional breakout trading. The analysis defined a hierarchical signal logic: first, a correlation gatekeeper (average inter-metal correlation <0.65); second, a volatility trigger (ATR ratio >1.2 on at least two metals); third, a direction selector using z-score ranking to sell the most extended metal and buy the most lagging, requiring a minimum spread of 1.5. Critical constraints included adapting the strategy for a small account by reducing position sizes to 0.01-0.03 lots, implementing strict basket-level risk management (1-2% risk per basket), and adding time-based exits. This evolved into building a free, manual trading system using MT5 as a data engine and a spreadsheet (Google Sheets/Excel) as a calculation dashboard, with precise formulas for correlation, ATR, z-scores, and a composite signal. Key decisions included rejecting a pure time-based breakout thesis in favor of a statistical dislocation model and creating a step-by-step checklist with helper columns for transparency. The technical implementation involved correcting critical formula errors, developing Google Apps Script for automated price fetching from Investing.com, and creating a secondary signal log sheet that filters trade signals, parses metal pairs, calculates position sizes, and derives entry, stop loss (2× ATR), and take profit levels for direct MT5 execution. Subsequently, we designed a comprehensive MetaTrader 5 Expert Advisor for backtesting and automated execution, incorporating volatility-normalized position sizing, explicit correlation regime filtering (requiring current average correlation to be in the bottom 30-40% of recent history), and basket-level exits with multiple conditions (primary: 45-50% Z-score spread compression; secondary: basket-level take-profit at 3% or stop-loss at -2.5%; tertiary: maximum 24-hour time exit). The EA is optimized for a $10,000-$50,000 account using 2% risk per basket trade. Backtesting recommendations include using M5/M15 timeframes, enabling multi-symbol testing, setting realistic commissions and slippage, and testing across various market regimes, targeting performance metrics of a 55-65% win rate, profit factor of 1.8-2.5, maximum drawdown below 20%, and Sharpe ratio above 1.2. An analysis of a failed EA (MetalsBasketEA_v3) backtest—showing a net profit of -466.43, profit factor of 0.50, and 5.38% win rate—informed critical refinements to our logic, emphasizing stricter entry filters, reduced trading frequency, and optimized exits. A subsequent review of a new backtest (MetalsBasketEA_v4) showed promising but sample-limited results over one month on M5: total net profit of 53.56 USD, profit factor of 2.53, max equity drawdown of 1.09%, but only 3 total trades (33.33% win rate) and a low Sharpe ratio of 0.21, highlighting the need for extended backtesting and potential entry condition optimization. Finally, we conducted a broker and prop firm analysis for VPS-hosted EA execution, identifying IC Markets and XM as top retail choices and FundedNext as the leading prop firm option with explicit EA support, providing a clear pathway for cost-effective live deployment after rigorous validation. The project state is a fully defined statistical strategy with a functional manual spreadsheet system and a specification for a refined MT5 EA, pending more extensive backtesting to confirm robustness across market conditions before live deployment. In parallel, we developed a streamlined Google Apps Script to automate the spreadsheet's price fetching and signal processing, consolidating functions to three core operations: updateAll() to fetch prices from FreeForexAPI (with Yahoo Finance fallback) and process signals, autoUpdate() to set 5-minute automated execution, and updateSignalLog() to process signals independently. Key technical corrections included fixing formula errors in Sheet1's signal column (L2) and ATR calculations (G2), ensuring proper data flow from Sheet1 (price data) to Sheet2 (signal log with entry, stop loss, and take profit calculations). The script now provides a complete, automated dashboard for manual trading signal generation, bridging the gap between the statistical strategy and executable trades in MT5.
